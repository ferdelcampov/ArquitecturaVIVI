Stoa of Attalos

A study of the Stoa of Attalos as an architectural instrument for public gathering, commerce, and civic life in the ancient Agora.

Ancient Agora Location
2 levels Structure
Public Use
Reconstruction Study type

Concept

A civic promenade shaped by architecture

The Stoa of Attalos is approached here as a layered architectural artifact that connects movement, commerce, and collective experience. The proposal focuses on how its colonnaded form organizes public life through rhythm, shelter, and a clear relationship between interior and exterior space.
